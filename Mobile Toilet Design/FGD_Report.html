Dhaka's rapid urbanization has outpaced its public sanitation infrastructure. For millions of commuters, vendors, and residents in low-income areas, the lack of safe and clean toilets is a daily crisis, particularly impacting the health, safety, and dignity of women and children.

What Do People Want? User Priorities

Surveys and user feedback reveal that functionality and safety are far more important than aesthetics. When people decide whether to use a public toilet, these are the factors that matter most.

Core Design & Feature Checklist

1. Hygiene & Amenities

  • Water Supply: Consistent running water is non-negotiable.
  • Hand Washing: Basin with soap or sanitizer dispenser.
  • Ventilation: Maximum airflow to control odor.
  • MHM: Proper disposal bins for menstrual products.
  • Flooring: Anti-slip, easy-to-clean surfaces.

2. Safety & Security

  • Lighting: Bright internal and external lighting.
  • Locking Mechanism: Secure, reliable inner latch with occupancy indicator.
  • Privacy: Opaque, sturdy cabin materials.
  • Location: Must be in a visible, well-trafficked area.

3. Accessibility for All

  • Ramp Access: Gentle slope for wheelchair entry.
  • Spacious Interior: Adequate room for a wheelchair to maneuver.
  • Grab Rails: Strategically placed for support.
  • Toilet Height: Accessible height commode option.

4. Cultural & Material Considerations

Toilet Type:

Provide both high-commode (sitting) and low-commode (squatting) options to cater to user preferences and cultural habits. Always include a hand shower or water source ('bodna') for personal cleansing.

Construction Materials:

Utilize durable, low-cost, and corrosion-resistant materials like Fiberglass Reinforced Plastic (FRP) or powder-coated steel. These materials withstand heavy use and are easy to sanitize, ensuring longevity and hygiene.

A Sustainable Operational Model

A successful mobile toilet project depends on a robust system for maintenance and waste management. A Public-Private Partnership (PPP) model, where the city provides oversight and a private entity manages daily operations, is highly recommended.
