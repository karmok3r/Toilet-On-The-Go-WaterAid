Re-thinking Sanitation

Essential Design Criteria for Mobile Toilets in the Bangladeshi Context

The Urgent Need for Better Solutions

In many urban areas of Bangladesh, the sanitation crisis is severe. Existing public facilities are overburdened and often inadequate, posing significant health and safety risks to the population.

50+

People Per Latrine

In densely populated urban informal settlements, a single toilet facility is often shared by over 50 individuals, leading to extreme overuse and poor hygiene.

66%

Urban Population Lack Safe Sanitation

Approximately two-thirds of the urban population in Bangladesh lack access to safely managed sanitation, increasing the risk of waterborne diseases.

A User-First Design Framework

Effective mobile toilets must be designed around the diverse needs of their users. Safety, privacy, and accessibility are not luxuries‚Äîthey are fundamental requirements for adoption and success.

User Priorities Ranked

When considering public toilets, users across all demographics consistently rank these factors as their top concerns. A design that fails to address these is destined for failure.

The Gender Safety Gap

Women and girls face disproportionate risks and require facilities that ensure their safety and cater to menstrual hygiene needs. The perception of safety is a major barrier to use.
